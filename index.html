I am an Associate Professor of Applied Psychology and Economics at NYU's Steinhardt School of Culture, Education, and Human Development, currently visiting the Harvard Graduate School of Education.

I pursue research to support low- and middle-income countries address the needs of the increasingly large, and growingly diverse student populations, from pre-primary to secondary education. You can read more here.

I hold an EdD in Quantitative Policy Analysis in Education from Harvard, where I was a fellow in the Multidisciplinary Program in Inequality and Social Policy; an MPhil from Cambridge, where I was a Gates Scholar; and a BSFS from Georgetown. I was a postdoctoral fellow at J-PAL.
